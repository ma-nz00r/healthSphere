Lou Gehrig's Disease (ALS)

Overview

ALS, or Lou Gehrig's Disease, is a progressive neurological disorder that affects nerve cells controlling muscles.

Causes

Most cases are sporadic; some linked to genetic mutations.

Symptoms

  • Muscle weakness
  • Difficulty speaking/swallowing
  • Paralysis in later stages

Treatment

No cure; medications like riluzole slow progression. Supportive care improves quality of life.

Prevention

No known prevention; genetic testing may help at-risk families.
