Hydrocephalus

Overview

Hydrocephalus is a condition in which excess cerebrospinal fluid builds up within the brain, increasing pressure.

Causes

  • Congenital malformations
  • Brain injury
  • Infections such as meningitis
  • Tumors blocking fluid pathways

Symptoms

  • Head enlargement in infants
  • Headaches
  • Vision problems
  • Balance issues
  • Cognitive difficulties

Treatment

Treated with surgical shunts or endoscopic third ventriculostomy (ETV).

Prevention

No complete prevention, but prenatal care and infection control reduce risks.
