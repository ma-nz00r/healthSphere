Emphysema

What is Emphysema?



Emphysema is a chronic lung condition that involves damage to the air sacs (alveoli) in the lungs.
This damage reduces the surface area for gas exchange, making it difficult for the lungs to supply oxygen to the blood and remove carbon dioxide.



Causes



Long-term exposure to cigarette smoke (primary cause).

Exposure to air pollution or harmful chemical fumes.

Chronic exposure to dust in certain occupational settings.

Genetic factors, such as alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency.

Symptoms



Shortness of breath, especially during physical activity.

Chronic cough.

Wheezing.

Fatigue.

Unintended weight loss.

Treatment



Quitting smoking to slow disease progression.

Using bronchodilators and inhalers to open airways.

Oxygen therapy for severe cases.

Pulmonary rehabilitation to improve lung function.

In advanced cases, lung surgery or transplantation may be considered.

Prevention



Avoid smoking or quit if you currently smoke.

Minimize exposure to air pollutants and chemical fumes.

Use protective equipment in hazardous work environments.

Maintain a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise and a balanced diet.
