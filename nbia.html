Neurodegeneration with Brain Iron Accumulation (NBIA)

Overview

NBIA is a rare group of inherited neurological disorders characterized by abnormal accumulation of iron in the brain, leading to progressive movement and cognitive problems.

Causes

Caused by genetic mutations affecting brain iron metabolism.

Symptoms

  • Movement difficulties (dystonia, tremors)
  • Muscle stiffness
  • Cognitive decline
  • Vision problems
  • Difficulty walking

Treatment

No cure; management includes medications, physical therapy, speech therapy, and supportive care.

Prevention

Cannot be prevented; genetic counseling is recommended for at-risk families.
