Myotonic Dystrophy

Overview

Myotonic dystrophy is a genetic disorder that causes progressive muscle weakness and difficulty relaxing muscles after contraction.

Causes

  • Genetic mutation in the DMPK or CNBP gene
  • Inherited in an autosomal dominant pattern

Symptoms

  • Muscle stiffness and weakness
  • Cataracts
  • Heart rhythm problems
  • Difficulty swallowing

Treatment

No cure. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms with physical therapy, medications, and cardiac monitoring.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for at-risk families
