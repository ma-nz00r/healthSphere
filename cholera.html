Cholera

Causes of Cholera



Cholera is caused by the bacterium

Vibrio cholerae,
which is typically spread through contaminated water or food. It can spread rapidly in areas where sanitation is poor.





Symptoms of Cholera



Severe diarrhea

Vomiting

Dehydration

Rapid heart rate

Low blood pressure

Muscle cramps



Treatment for Cholera



Immediate rehydration through oral rehydration salts (ORS) or intravenous fluids is essential. In some cases, antibiotics may be used to shorten the duration of the disease.





Prevention of Cholera



Improving water and sanitation systems

Boiling or disinfecting drinking water

Proper hygiene, including regular hand washing

Vaccination (oral cholera vaccines)
