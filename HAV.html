Hepatitis A

Overview

Hepatitis A is a viral infection that affects the liver. It spreads mainly through contaminated food, water, or close contact with an infected person. Most people recover fully without lasting liver damage.

Causes

  • Contaminated food or water
  • Close contact with an infected person
  • Poor sanitation and hygiene

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and loss of appetite
  • Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes)
  • Abdominal pain & nausea
  • Dark urine, pale stools
  • Fever and joint pain

Treatment

No specific cure exists. Care focuses on rest, hydration, balanced diet, and avoiding alcohol. Most patients recover in weeks to months.

Prevention

  • Vaccination
  • Handwashing & good hygiene
  • Safe food and clean water
  • Avoid close contact with infected people
