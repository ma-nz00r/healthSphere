Myasthenia Gravis

Overview

Myasthenia gravis is a chronic autoimmune neuromuscular disorder that causes weakness in skeletal muscles.

Causes

  • Autoimmune attack on acetylcholine receptors at neuromuscular junction
  • Sometimes associated with thymus gland abnormalities

Symptoms

  • Drooping eyelids
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Weakness in arms and legs
  • Breathing difficulties in severe cases

Treatment

Anticholinesterase medications, immunosuppressants, plasma exchange, and sometimes thymectomy.

Prevention

  • No prevention, but treatment improves quality of life
