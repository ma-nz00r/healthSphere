Brucellosis

Brucellosis Overview


Brucellosis is a bacterial infection with zoonotic transmission, primarily occurring through contact with infected livestock (cattle, sheep, goats, pigs), consumption of unpasteurized dairy or undercooked meat, inhalation of contaminated aerosols (occupational), or direct contact with infected animal tissues.

Clinical Presentation


Symptoms exhibit variability and may include fever, chills, night sweats, fatigue, myalgia, arthralgia, headache, anorexia, abdominal pain, and lymphadenopathy or splenomegaly. Symptom onset can range from days to weeks post-exposure.

Therapeutic Strategies


Treatment necessitates a prolonged antibiotic regimen, typically involving a combination of doxycycline with rifampin or streptomycin, administered over several weeks to months for complete bacterial eradication. Symptomatic management may also be required.

Preventive Measures


Prevention strategies include avoiding unpasteurized dairy and undercooked meat, utilizing personal protective equipment when handling animals or animal products, livestock vaccination, diligent hand hygiene following animal contact, and adherence to strict safety protocols in high-risk occupational settings.
