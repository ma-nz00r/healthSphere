Fascioliasis

Definition



Fascioliasis is a parasitic disease caused by liver flukes, primarily Fasciola hepatica and Fasciola gigantica.
These parasites mainly infect the liver and bile ducts of humans and animals.



Causes



Consumption of raw or undercooked aquatic plants (e.g., watercress) contaminated with fluke larvae.

Drinking contaminated water containing parasite larvae.

Symptoms



Fever

Abdominal pain, especially in the upper right side

Nausea and vomiting

Diarrhea

Loss of appetite

Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)

Treatment



The primary treatment for fascioliasis is the use of antiparasitic medication, such as
triclabendazole. In some cases, supportive care, including pain relief and hydration, may be necessary.



Prevention



Avoid eating raw or undercooked aquatic plants

Drink clean, safe, and properly treated water

Improve sanitation and hygiene practices to reduce contamination
