Intraocular Melanoma

Overview

Intraocular Melanoma is the most common eye cancer in adults, arising from pigment-producing cells.

Causes

Linked to UV exposure, fair skin, and genetic mutations.

Symptoms

  • Blurred vision
  • Dark spots on the iris
  • Loss of peripheral vision

Treatment

Options include radiation therapy, laser treatment, or surgery depending on tumor size.

Prevention

Wearing UV-protective sunglasses and regular eye exams help reduce risk.
