X-Linked Lymphoproliferative Disease

Overview

A rare inherited immunodeficiency disorder affecting boys, leading to abnormal immune responses, especially to Epstein-Barr virus (EBV).

Causes

  • Mutation in SH2D1A gene on the X chromosome.
  • X-linked recessive inheritance pattern.

Symptoms

  • Severe infectious mononucleosis.
  • Immune system dysfunction and lymphoma risk.
  • Enlarged liver, spleen, or lymph nodes.

Treatment

  • Bone marrow or stem cell transplantation.
  • Immunoglobulin replacement therapy.
  • Supportive care for infections.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for families at risk.
  • Early diagnosis and monitoring to reduce complications.
