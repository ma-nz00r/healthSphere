Malaria

Overview

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through mosquito bites, especially in tropical and subtropical regions.

Causes

  • Plasmodium parasites (P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. malariae, P. ovale)
  • Spread through Anopheles mosquito bites

Symptoms

  • High fever
  • Chills and sweats
  • Headaches and nausea
  • Muscle pain and fatigue

Treatment

Antimalarial drugs such as artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs) are effective in treating malaria.

Prevention

  • Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets
  • Indoor spraying with insecticides
  • Preventive antimalarial drugs in high-risk areas
