Kleine-Levin Syndrome

Overview

Kleine-Levin Syndrome is a rare neurological disorder characterized by recurring episodes of excessive sleep, altered behavior, and reduced understanding of the world.

Causes

The cause is unclear but may involve hypothalamic dysfunction, autoimmune response, or genetics.

Symptoms

  • Sleeping up to 20 hours a day
  • Confusion and disorientation
  • Increased appetite and hypersexuality

Treatment

No cure; stimulants and mood stabilizers may help reduce episodes.

Prevention

No prevention available; early recognition helps in management.
