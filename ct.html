CT(Computed Tomography) Scan

You can get CT(Computed Tomography) Scan services
from this site.

A CT (computed tomography) scan is a type of imaging test. Like an X-ray, it shows structures inside your body. But instead of creating a flat, 2D image, a CT scan takes dozens to hundreds of images of your body. To get these images, a CT machine takes X-ray pictures as it revolves around you. Healthcare providers use CT scans to see things that regular X-rays can’t show. For example, body structures overlap on regular X-rays and many things aren’t visible. A CT shows the details of each of your organs for a clearer and more precise view.

A CT scan takes pictures of your:
Bones
Muscles
Organs
Blood vessels

CT scans help healthcare providers detect various
injuries and diseases, including:

Certain types of cancer and benign tumors.
Fractures (broken bones)
Heart disease
Blood clots
Bowel disorders
Kidney stones
Brain injuries
Spinal cord injuries
Internal bleeding
