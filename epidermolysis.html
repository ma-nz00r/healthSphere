Epidermolysis Bullosa (EB)

Definition



Epidermolysis Bullosa (EB) is a group of rare genetic conditions that cause the skin to become very fragile and blister easily.
Even minor friction or trauma can lead to painful blisters and skin damage.



Causes



EB is caused by genetic mutations that affect the proteins responsible for skin strength and integrity. These mutations can
be inherited in an autosomal dominant or recessive manner, depending on the type of EB.



Symptoms



Common symptoms of EB include:



Fragile skin that blisters easily

Open wounds and sores

Thickened skin on the palms and soles

Difficulty swallowing (in severe cases)

Scarring and deformities

Treatment



There is no cure for EB, but treatment focuses on managing symptoms and preventing complications. This may include:



Wound care and bandaging

Pain management

Nutritional support

Physical therapy

Surgical interventions in severe cases

Prevention



Since EB is a genetic condition, it cannot be prevented. However, genetic counseling can help families understand the risks
and implications of passing the condition to future generations. Preventive care focuses on minimizing skin trauma and maintaining overall health.
