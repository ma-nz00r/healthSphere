Fragile X Syndrome

What is Fragile X Syndrome?



Fragile X Syndrome is a genetic condition causing intellectual disability, behavioral and learning challenges, and various
physical characteristics. It is the most common inherited cause of intellectual disability and is linked to the X chromosome.



Causes



Fragile X Syndrome is caused by a mutation in the FMR1 gene located on the X chromosome. This gene mutation leads to the
absence or reduction of the FMRP protein, which is essential for normal brain development.



Symptoms



Intellectual disability (mild to moderate)

Social anxiety and social withdrawal

Hyperactivity and impulsive behavior

Speech and language delays

Repetitive behaviors, such as hand-flapping

Autistic-like behaviors

Physical characteristics, such as a long face and large ears

Treatment



Currently, there is no cure for Fragile X Syndrome. However, treatment focuses on managing symptoms and providing support.
Some common treatments include:



Behavioral therapy

Speech therapy

Occupational therapy

Medications to manage symptoms (e.g., antidepressants, stimulants)

Prevention



Since Fragile X Syndrome is a genetic disorder, prevention is not possible. However, genetic counseling can help
families understand the risks of passing on the mutation and provide information about prenatal testing options.
