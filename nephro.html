Nephrotic Syndrome

Overview

Nephrotic Syndrome is a kidney disorder that causes the body to excrete too much protein in the urine.

Causes

Caused by damage to the kidneyâ€™s filtering units (glomeruli). May result from diabetes, lupus, infections, or genetic conditions.

Symptoms

  • Swelling (edema), especially around eyes and ankles
  • Foamy urine
  • Weight gain due to fluid retention
  • Fatigue and loss of appetite

Treatment

Treatment includes corticosteroids, diuretics, blood pressure medications, and dietary changes.

Prevention

Prevention focuses on managing conditions like diabetes and high blood pressure and regular kidney function monitoring.
