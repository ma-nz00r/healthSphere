Anthrax

Overview

Anthrax is a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Bacillus anthracis. It mainly affects animals but can also infect humans through direct or indirect contact.

Causes

Anthrax is caused by spores of Bacillus anthracis. Humans can contract it through inhalation, ingestion, or skin contact with contaminated animal products.

Symptoms

  • Skin blisters or sores (cutaneous anthrax)
  • Severe difficulty breathing (inhalation anthrax)
  • Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain (gastrointestinal anthrax)
  • Fever and chills
  • Shock in severe cases

Prevention

Preventive measures include vaccination for at-risk individuals, avoiding contact with infected animals, and proper handling of animal products.

Treatment

Anthrax can be treated with antibiotics and supportive care. Early medical attention is crucial for survival, especially in inhalation anthrax.
