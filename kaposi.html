Kaposi's Sarcoma

Overview

Kaposi's Sarcoma is a cancer that develops in blood vessel linings, often seen in people with weakened immune systems.

Causes

Caused by infection with HHV-8 virus, especially in HIV/AIDS patients.

Symptoms

  • Purple or red skin lesions
  • Swelling in legs
  • Oral or internal lesions

Treatment

Treatment includes antiretroviral therapy, chemotherapy, radiation depending on severity.

Prevention

Preventing HIV and maintaining immune health lowers risk.
