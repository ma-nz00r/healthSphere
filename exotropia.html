Exotropia

What is Exotropia?



Exotropia is a type of strabismus, or eye misalignment, where one or both eyes turn outward.
It can occur intermittently or be constant, and it often becomes more noticeable when a person is tired, stressed, or focusing on distant objects.



Causes



Exotropia can result from a variety of factors, including:



Genetic predisposition

Weakness in eye muscles

Neurological or developmental issues

Eye injuries or other medical conditions

Symptoms



Common symptoms of exotropia include:



Outward turning of one or both eyes

Double vision

Difficulty focusing

Frequent squinting or closing one eye in bright sunlight

Headaches or eye strain

Treatment



Treatment for exotropia may involve one or more of the following approaches:



Prescription glasses or contact lenses

Vision therapy to strengthen eye muscles

Eye patching to encourage the weaker eye to work harder

Surgery to correct eye alignment in severe cases

Prevention



While not all cases of exotropia can be prevented, you can reduce the risk or severity by:



Regular eye exams, especially for children

Prompt treatment of any vision issues

Protecting eyes from injury

Encouraging healthy visual habits, such as taking breaks from screens
