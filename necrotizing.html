Necrotizing Fasciitis

Overview

Necrotizing Fasciitis is a rare but severe bacterial infection that destroys soft tissue beneath the skin. It spreads rapidly and requires urgent medical treatment.

Causes

Caused by bacteria such as Group A Streptococcus, Staphylococcus, or mixed bacterial infections. It often enters through cuts, wounds, or surgical incisions.

Symptoms

  • Severe pain and swelling at the infection site
  • Red or purple skin discoloration
  • Fever and chills
  • Blisters and ulcers
  • Tissue death (necrosis)

Treatment

Treatment includes intravenous antibiotics, surgical removal of dead tissue, and in some cases, amputation. Early diagnosis is critical.

Prevention

Good wound care, proper hygiene, and avoiding swimming in contaminated water with open wounds can reduce the risk.
