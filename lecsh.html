Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome

Overview

Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder affecting metabolism and causing neurological problems.

Causes

Caused by mutations in the HPRT1 gene, leading to uric acid buildup.

Symptoms

  • Self-injurious behaviors
  • Motor impairment
  • Kidney problems

Treatment

No cure; treatments include allopurinol to reduce uric acid and therapies for behavior control.

Prevention

Genetic counseling is important for at-risk families.
