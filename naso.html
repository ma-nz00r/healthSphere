Nasopharyngeal Cancer

Overview

Nasopharyngeal Cancer is a rare type of cancer that forms in the nasopharynx, located behind the nose and above the throat.

Causes

Linked to Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection, genetic predisposition, and environmental factors such as high consumption of preserved foods.

Symptoms

  • Lump in the neck
  • Nasal congestion or bleeding
  • Hearing loss or ringing in the ears
  • Headaches
  • Facial pain or numbness

Treatment

Treatment includes radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and in some cases, surgery.

Prevention

Reducing risk factors such as avoiding preserved foods, stopping smoking, and early screening for high-risk individuals.
