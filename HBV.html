Hepatitis B (HBV)

Overview

Hepatitis B is a viral infection that attacks the liver and can cause chronic disease.

Causes

  • Transmission through blood and body fluids
  • Unprotected sexual contact
  • Sharing needles
  • Mother to child during childbirth

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Jaundice (yellow eyes/skin)
  • Abdominal pain
  • Nausea and vomiting

Treatment

No complete cure, but antiviral medications help control infection and prevent complications.

Prevention

  • Vaccination
  • Safe sex practices
  • Avoid sharing needles
