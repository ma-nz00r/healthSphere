Menkes Disease

Overview

Menkes disease is a rare inherited disorder affecting copper levels in the body, leading to neurological and connective tissue problems.

Causes

  • Mutation in ATP7A gene
  • Leads to impaired copper absorption and transport

Symptoms

  • Seizures
  • Weak muscle tone
  • Characteristic "kinky" hair
  • Developmental delays

Treatment

Early copper injections may improve outcomes. Supportive therapies include seizure management and nutritional support.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for families with history of Menkes disease
