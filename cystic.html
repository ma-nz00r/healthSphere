Cystic Firbrosis

Causes of Cystic Fibrosis



Cystic fibrosis is caused by mutations in the

Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Conductance Regulator (CFTR) gene. This gene helps regulate the flow of salt and fluids in and out of cells.
When the gene is defective, it leads to the production of thick, sticky mucus that can clog the lungs and digestive system.



Symptoms of Cystic Fibrosis



Chronic cough with mucus

Shortness of breath

Wheezing and lung infections

Poor growth or weight gain

Difficulty digesting food and poor nutrition

Frequent salty-tasting skin

Treatment for Cystic Fibrosis



While there is no cure for cystic fibrosis, treatments are available to help manage the symptoms and improve quality of life. These include:



Aerosol therapy and inhaled medications to loosen mucus

Chest physical therapy (CPT) to clear lung passages

Enzyme replacement for digestive issues

Lung transplants in severe cases

Antibiotics to prevent and treat lung infections



Prevention of Cystic Fibrosis



Since cystic fibrosis is a genetic condition, it cannot be prevented. However, early diagnosis through newborn screening can
help in managing the disease. Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of cystic fibrosis.
