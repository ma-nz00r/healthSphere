Guillain-Barré Syndrome

Overview

Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS) is a rare neurological disorder in which the immune system attacks the nerves.

Causes

Often triggered by infections such as influenza, Zika virus, or Campylobacter bacteria.

Symptoms

  • Weakness in legs and arms
  • Tingling sensations
  • Difficulty breathing in severe cases
  • Loss of reflexes

Treatment

Plasma exchange (plasmapheresis), intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG), and supportive care.

Prevention

No guaranteed prevention, but prompt treatment of infections may reduce risks.
