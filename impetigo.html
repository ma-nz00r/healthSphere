Impetigo

Overview

Impetigo is a common bacterial skin infection, often seen in children, causing red sores and crusts.

Causes

Caused by Staphylococcus aureus or Streptococcus pyogenes bacteria.

Symptoms

  • Red sores that burst and form yellow-brown crust
  • Itching and discomfort
  • Spreading rash

Treatment

Topical or oral antibiotics are used, along with good skin hygiene.

Prevention

Frequent handwashing, avoid sharing towels, and keep wounds clean.
