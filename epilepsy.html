Epilepsy

What is Epilepsy?



Epilepsy is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, unprovoked seizures. It occurs due to
abnormal electrical activity in the brain.



Causes



The causes of epilepsy can vary and may include:



Genetic factors

Head trauma

Brain conditions such as tumors or strokes

Infectious diseases like meningitis

Prenatal injury

Developmental disorders

Symptoms



Common symptoms of epilepsy include:



Temporary confusion

Uncontrollable jerking movements

Loss of consciousness or awareness

Psychological symptoms such as fear or anxiety

Treatment



Treatment options for epilepsy include:



Medications to control seizures

Surgical procedures

Therapies such as vagus nerve stimulation

Specialized diets like the ketogenic diet

Prevention



While epilepsy cannot always be prevented, some steps can reduce the risk:



Wearing helmets to prevent head injuries

Managing chronic conditions like hypertension

Practicing good prenatal care

Avoiding brain infections through proper hygiene
