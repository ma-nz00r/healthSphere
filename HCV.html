Hepatitis C (HCV)

Overview

Hepatitis C is a viral infection that primarily affects the liver and can lead to cirrhosis or liver cancer.

Causes

  • Blood-to-blood contact
  • Unsafe injections
  • Sharing contaminated needles
  • Rarely from sexual contact

Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Dark urine
  • Joint pain
  • Loss of appetite

Treatment

HCV is treated with direct-acting antivirals (DAAs), which can cure most cases.

Prevention

  • No vaccine available
  • Avoid sharing needles
  • Safe medical practices
