Hurler Syndrome

Overview

Hurler Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder caused by enzyme deficiency, leading to buildup of harmful substances in the body.

Causes

Caused by deficiency of the enzyme alpha-L-iduronidase leading to accumulation of glycosaminoglycans.

Symptoms

  • Coarse facial features
  • Enlarged organs
  • Developmental delay
  • Skeletal deformities

Treatment

Options include enzyme replacement therapy and bone marrow transplantation.

Prevention

No prevention, but genetic counseling can help at-risk families.
