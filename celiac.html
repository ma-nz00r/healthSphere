Celiac Disease

Causes of Celiac Disease



Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder in which the ingestion of gluten (a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye) causes
damage to the small intestine. The exact cause is not fully understood, but the following factors are believed to contribute:



Genetic predisposition (family history of celiac disease or other autoimmune disorders)

Abnormal immune system response to gluten, which damages the intestinal lining

Environmental triggers such as infections, stress, or surgery

Presence of other autoimmune diseases like Type 1 diabetes or autoimmune thyroid disease

Symptoms of Celiac Disease



Symptoms can vary from mild to severe and may affect various parts of the body. Common symptoms include:



Chronic diarrhea or constipation

Abdominal pain, bloating, or cramping

Fatigue or weakness

Unexplained weight loss

Skin rashes (Dermatitis herpetiformis)

Iron-deficiency anemia

Delayed growth or development in children

Joint pain or bone disorders

Treatment for Celiac Disease



The only effective treatment for celiac disease is a strict, lifelong gluten-free diet. By avoiding foods containing gluten, individuals can
manage symptoms and prevent further damage to the intestines. Additional treatments may include:



Gluten-free diet: Strict avoidance of foods containing wheat, barley, and rye

Supplements: Iron, calcium, and vitamin D supplements to address deficiencies

Medications: In some cases, corticosteroids or other immune-modulating drugs may be prescribed to reduce inflammation and manage symptoms

Monitoring and follow-up: Regular check-ups to monitor intestinal health and screen for other complications

Prevention of Celiac Disease



There is no known way to prevent celiac disease, as it is primarily influenced by genetic factors. However, early diagnosis and adhering to
a gluten-free diet can prevent the progression of the disease and help manage symptoms effectively. Tips for managing celiac disease include:



Early screening for those with a family history of celiac disease or other autoimmune conditions

Educating and training individuals on how to maintain a gluten-free lifestyle

Being vigilant about hidden sources of gluten in processed foods and medications

Maintaining a balanced diet with proper nutrient intake (iron, calcium, vitamins) to support overall health
