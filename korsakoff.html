Korsakoff's Syndrome

Overview

Korsakoff's Syndrome is a chronic memory disorder caused by severe deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1).

Causes

Most commonly associated with long-term alcohol misuse and poor nutrition.

Symptoms

  • Severe memory loss
  • Confabulation (making up stories)
  • Difficulty learning new information

Treatment

Treatment involves thiamine supplements, alcohol cessation, and nutritional support.

Prevention

Avoiding alcohol abuse and ensuring adequate vitamin intake prevents the disorder.
