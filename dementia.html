Dementia

Causes



Dementia is caused by damage to brain cells, which interferes with their ability to communicate. Common causes include:



Alzheimer's disease

Vascular dementia

Lewy body dementia

Frontotemporal dementia

Infections or head injuries

Symptoms



Common symptoms of dementia include:



Memory loss

Difficulty communicating

Problems with reasoning and judgment

Changes in mood and behavior

Confusion and disorientation

Treatment



While there is no cure for most types of dementia, treatments can help manage symptoms:



Medications such as cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine

Therapies, including cognitive stimulation and occupational therapy

Lifestyle adjustments to create a supportive environment

Management of underlying conditions

Prevention



Some measures may help reduce the risk of dementia:



Maintaining a healthy diet

Exercising regularly

Keeping mentally and socially active

Controlling risk factors such as high blood pressure and diabetes

Getting adequate sleep
