Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL)

Overview

Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL) is a type of cancer of the blood and bone marrow that affects white blood cells. It progresses rapidly and creates immature lymphocytes that crowd out normal cells, leading to anemia, infection, and bleeding issues.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations affecting blood cell production
  • Exposure to high doses of radiation
  • Chemical exposure (e.g., benzene)
  • Family history of leukemia or genetic disorders

Symptoms

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Frequent infections
  • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • Bone or joint pain
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Fever or night sweats

Treatment

  • Chemotherapy to kill leukemia cells
  • Radiation therapy in some cases
  • Stem cell or bone marrow transplantation
  • Targeted therapy using medications that attack specific cancer cells
  • Supportive care to manage symptoms and prevent infection

Prevention

  • There is no guaranteed prevention
  • Minimizing exposure to radiation and harmful chemicals
  • Regular medical check-ups for those with family history
  • Healthy lifestyle to support overall immune function
