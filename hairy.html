Hairy Cell Leukemia

Overview

Hairy Cell Leukemia is a rare type of blood cancer that affects B lymphocytes, causing abnormal growth of white blood cells.

Causes

The cause is unclear but associated with genetic changes in BRAF gene.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Frequent infections
  • Easy bruising
  • Swollen spleen

Treatment

Managed with chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and sometimes immunotherapy.

Prevention

No prevention methods exist, but early medical care helps manage symptoms.
