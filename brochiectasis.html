Bronchiectasis

Causes of Bronchiectasis



Bronchiectasis is caused by damage to the airways that leads to chronic inflammation. Some of the primary causes include:



Chronic respiratory infections

Genetic conditions like cystic fibrosis

Immune system disorders

Airway obstructions

Exposure to toxic substances

Symptoms of Bronchiectasis



Common symptoms of bronchiectasis include:



Chronic cough

Excessive mucus production

Shortness of breath

Fatigue

Frequent respiratory infections

Treatment of Bronchiectasis



The treatment plan for bronchiectasis may vary based on the severity and underlying cause. Common treatment options include:



Antibiotics for infections

Airway clearance techniques

Bronchodilators

Inhaled corticosteroids

Oxygen therapy (for severe cases)



Prevention of Bronchiectasis



Preventive measures to reduce the risk of bronchiectasis include:



Vaccination to prevent respiratory infections

Quitting smoking

Regular hand washing to avoid infections

Regular medical check-ups for people at risk
