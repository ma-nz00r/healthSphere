Muscular Dystrophy

Overview

Muscular dystrophy is a group of genetic diseases that cause progressive weakness and loss of muscle mass.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations affecting muscle proteins
  • Often inherited from parents

Symptoms

  • Muscle weakness
  • Difficulty walking
  • Frequent falls
  • Breathing and heart complications

Treatment

No cure. Treatments include physical therapy, medications, breathing assistance, and surgery to manage complications.

Prevention

  • Genetic counseling for at-risk families
