Isovaleric Acidemia

Overview

Isovaleric Acidemia is a rare genetic metabolic disorder where the body cannot properly break down leucine.

Causes

Caused by mutations in the IVD gene, leading to a buildup of toxic isovaleric acid.

Symptoms

  • Vomiting
  • Lethargy
  • Seizures
  • “Sweaty feet” odor

Treatment

Diet low in leucine, glycine supplements, and emergency management during illness.

Prevention

Genetic screening, newborn screening, and dietary management can prevent severe outcomes.
