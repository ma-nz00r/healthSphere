Keratitis

Overview

Keratitis is inflammation of the cornea, the clear front part of the eye, which can threaten vision if untreated.

Causes

Can be caused by infections (bacterial, viral, fungal), injury, or improper contact lens use.

Symptoms

  • Eye pain and redness
  • Blurred vision
  • Excessive tearing
  • Sensitivity to light

Treatment

Treatment depends on cause: antibiotic, antiviral, or antifungal eye drops; in severe cases, corneal transplant.

Prevention

Proper eye hygiene and safe contact lens use help prevent keratitis.
