Neurofibromatosis

Overview

Neurofibromatosis (NF) is a genetic disorder that causes tumors to form on nerve tissue. It can affect the brain, spinal cord, and skin.

Causes

Caused by mutations in genes such as NF1, NF2, or SMARCB1. It is usually inherited but can also result from new mutations.

Treatment

No cure exists, but management includes surgery to remove tumors, medications to control pain, and genetic counseling.

Prevention

Prevention is not possible since it is genetic, but early diagnosis and monitoring can help manage complications.
