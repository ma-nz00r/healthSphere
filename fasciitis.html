Fasciitis

Definition

Fasciitis is the inflammation of the fascia, a band or sheet of connective tissue primarily made of collagen,
that lies beneath the skin and surrounds muscles, bones, and organs. The most common type is plantar fasciitis, which affects the foot.



Causes



Overuse or repetitive strain on the fascia

Injury or trauma

Infections (e.g., necrotizing fasciitis caused by bacteria)

Autoimmune disorders

Obesity or poor biomechanics

Symptoms



Pain and tenderness in the affected area

Swelling and redness

Difficulty moving the affected body part

Warmth over the inflamed area

Severe cases may involve fever or spreading infection

Treatment



Rest and avoidance of activities that worsen symptoms

Physical therapy to stretch and strengthen the affected area

Medications like NSAIDs (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs)

Injections (e.g., corticosteroids) for severe pain

Surgery in extreme or life-threatening cases

Antibiotics for infection-related fasciitis

Prevention



Maintain a healthy weight to reduce strain on the fascia

Wear supportive footwear, especially during physical activity

Stretch regularly, particularly before and after exercise

Avoid repetitive activities that strain specific body partsi

Practice good hygiene to prevent infections
