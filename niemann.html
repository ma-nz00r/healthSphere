Niemann-Pick Disease

Overview

Niemann-Pick Disease is a group of rare inherited metabolic disorders where harmful levels of lipids accumulate in the spleen, liver, lungs, bone marrow, and brain.

Causes

Caused by genetic mutations affecting lipid metabolism, specifically mutations in the SMPD1 or NPC1/NPC2 genes.

Symptoms

  • Enlarged liver and spleen
  • Difficulty feeding in infants
  • Progressive neurological decline
  • Loss of muscle control
  • Lung problems

Treatment

No complete cure exists, but supportive care, physical therapy, and medications like miglustat can slow disease progression.

Prevention

Genetic counseling and carrier screening for families at risk are the main preventive measures.
