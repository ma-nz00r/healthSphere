Kawasaki Disease

Overview

Kawasaki Disease is a childhood illness that causes inflammation in blood vessels and can affect the heart.

Causes

The exact cause is unknown, but it may involve immune system overreaction triggered by infections or genetics.

Symptoms

  • Fever lasting more than 5 days
  • Red eyes, lips, and tongue
  • Rash
  • Swollen hands and feet

Treatment

Treated with IV immunoglobulin (IVIG) and aspirin to reduce inflammation and prevent heart issues.

Prevention

No known prevention, but early treatment reduces complications.
