Lassa Fever

Overview

Lassa Fever is a viral hemorrhagic illness found in West Africa, transmitted by rodents and human-to-human contact.

Causes

Caused by Lassa virus, spread through exposure to rodent urine, feces, or infected people.

Symptoms

  • Fever and weakness
  • Headache and sore throat
  • Vomiting and diarrhea
  • Bleeding in severe cases

Treatment

Ribavirin may help if given early. Supportive care like hydration and oxygen therapy is vital.

Prevention

Prevent by rodent control, hygiene practices, and avoiding contact with infected individuals.
