E. coli

Causes



E. coli infection is caused by various strains of the Escherichia coli bacteria, which can be found in the environment, foods, and intestines of humans and animals. Common sources of infection include:



Contaminated food (e.g., undercooked ground beef, raw vegetables)

Unpasteurized milk or juice

Contaminated water

Close contact with infected individuals or animals

Symptoms



Symptoms of E. coli infection can vary but often include:



Diarrhea (sometimes bloody)

Stomach cramps

Nausea and vomiting

Fever

Fatigue

Treatment



There is no specific treatment for E. coli infections. Supportive care is key and may include:



Staying hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids

Avoiding anti-diarrheal medications, which can slow recovery

Seeking medical attention if symptoms are severe or prolonged

Prevention



To reduce the risk of E. coli infection, follow these prevention tips:



Cook meat thoroughly, especially ground beef

Wash fruits and vegetables before eating

Avoid cross-contamination in the kitchen

Drink pasteurized milk and juices

Wash hands frequently, especially after using the bathroom or handling raw food
