Ebola



Causes



Ebola is caused by the Ebola virus, which is transmitted to humans through close contact with infected animals
such as fruit bats or primates, or through contact with the bodily fluids of infected individuals.



Symptoms



Symptoms of Ebola usually appear 2 to 21 days after exposure and may include:



Fever

Severe headache

Muscle pain

> Weakness

Fatigue

Diarrhe

Vomiting

Abdominal pain

Unexplained bleeding or bruising

Treatment



Currently, there is no specific treatment for Ebola. Supportive care, including rehydration and treating specific
symptoms, can improve survival rates. Some antiviral medications and monoclonal antibodies have shown promise and are used in certain cases.



Prevention



To prevent Ebola, follow these measures:



    Avoid contact with infected individuals or their bodily fluids.

    Practice good hand hygiene by washing hands with soap and water.

    Avoid handling wild animals or their raw meat.

    Use protective equipment if caring for infected patients.

    Stay informed and follow public health advice during outbreaks.
