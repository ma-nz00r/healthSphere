Kabuki Syndrome

Overview

Kabuki Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder affecting facial features, growth, and intellectual development.

Causes

Caused by mutations in KMT2D or KDM6A genes.

Symptoms

  • Distinct facial features
  • Developmental delay
  • Heart defects
  • Joint laxity

Treatment

No cure; supportive therapies, surgery, and educational support are used.

Prevention

Genetic counseling helps at-risk families.
