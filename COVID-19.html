COVID-19

Causes of COVID-19



COVID-19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Some of the key transmission methods include:



Close contact with infected individuals

Touching contaminated surfaces and then touching the face

Inhalation of respiratory droplets in enclosed spaces

Symptoms of COVID-19



COVID-19 symptoms vary in severity, and some individuals may remain asymptomatic. Common symptoms include:



Fever or chills

Cough

Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing

Fatigue

Loss of taste or smell

Sore throat

Muscle or body aches

Headache

Treatment for COVID-19



There is no specific cure for COVID-19, but treatment focuses on relieving symptoms and supporting the bodyâ€™s immune response. Some common treatment methods include:



Antiviral medications (e.g., Remdesivir)

Oxygen therapy (for severe cases)

Steroids (e.g., Dexamethasone) to reduce inflammation

Supportive care, including hydration and rest

Hospitalization for severe cases requiring intensive care

Prevention of COVID-19



To reduce the risk of contracting or spreading COVID-19, it is important to follow prevention strategies such as:



Vaccination (available for most populations)

Wearing a mask, especially in crowded or indoor settings

Frequent hand washing with soap and water or using hand sanitizer

Maintaining physical distance from others (at least 6 feet)

Avoiding large gatherings

Quarantining if exposed or showing symptoms
