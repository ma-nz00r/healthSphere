Infective Endocarditis

Overview

Infective Endocarditis is an infection of the inner lining of the heart chambers and valves, usually caused by bacteria.

Causes

  • Bacterial infection entering bloodstream
  • Use of contaminated needles
  • Pre-existing heart valve disease

Symptoms

  • Fever and chills
  • Heart murmur
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Skin spots or rashes

Treatment

Treated with antibiotics for several weeks; severe cases may need heart valve surgery.

Prevention

Practice good dental hygiene, avoid contaminated needles, and take prophylactic antibiotics if at high risk.
