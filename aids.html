AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome)

Overview

AIDS is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). By damaging your immune system, HIV interferes with your body's ability to fight infections and diseases.

Causes

AIDS is caused by HIV. The virus is transmitted through contact with infected blood, semen, or vaginal fluids. Common ways of transmission include unprotected sex, sharing needles, or from mother to child during childbirth.

Symptoms

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Recurring fever or night sweats
  • Prolonged swelling of lymph glands
  • Frequent infections

Prevention

While there is no cure, HIV infection can be prevented by practicing safe sex, avoiding sharing needles, and taking preventive medications like PrEP.

Treatment

Antiretroviral therapy (ART) is the primary treatment for HIV/AIDS. It helps control the virus, improves quality of life, and reduces the risk of transmission.
