Ependymoma

Definition


Ependymoma is a rare type of tumor that arises from ependymal cells in the brain and spinal cord. These tumors
can occur at any age but are more common in children.



Causes



The exact cause of ependymoma is not fully understood. Genetic mutations and changes in cellular DNA are believed
to play a role in the development of these tumors.



Symptoms



Symptoms of ependymoma vary depending on the tumor's location but may include:



Headaches

Nausea and vomiting

Seizures

Weakness or numbness in the limbs

Difficulty with balance and coordination

Treatment



Treatment options for ependymoma include:



Surgical removal of the tumor

Radiation therapy to target remaining tumor cells

Chemotherapy in certain cases

The treatment plan depends on the tumor's size, location, and grade.



Prevention

Currently, there are no known ways to prevent ependymoma. Early detection and prompt treatment are crucial for better outcomes.
