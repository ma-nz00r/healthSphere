Achondroplasia

Overview

Achondroplasia is a rare genetic disorder that results in dwarfism. It affects bone growth, particularly in the arms and legs, leading to shorter stature while the trunk remains relatively normal in size.

Causes

This condition is caused by a mutation in the FGFR3 gene, which plays a role in converting cartilage into bone. The mutation leads to abnormal bone development and growth.

Symptoms

  • Short arms and legs with normal-sized torso
  • Large head with prominent forehead
  • Flattened nasal bridge
  • Limited range of motion at the elbows
  • Spinal curvature (lordosis or kyphosis)

Treatment

There is no cure for achondroplasia, but treatments can help manage complications. These include physical therapy, surgery for bone alignment, and medications to improve bone growth in children.

Prevention

Since achondroplasia is a genetic condition, there is no known prevention. However, genetic counseling can help families understand their risks and options.
