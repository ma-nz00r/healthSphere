Narcolepsy

Overview

Narcolepsy is a chronic sleep disorder characterized by overwhelming daytime drowsiness and sudden attacks of sleep.

Causes

Believed to result from a deficiency of hypocretin, a brain chemical that regulates wakefulness. Genetic and autoimmune factors may contribute.

Symptoms

  • Excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Sudden muscle weakness (cataplexy)
  • Sleep paralysis
  • Hallucinations when falling asleep or waking up

Treatment

Treatment includes stimulant medications, antidepressants, and lifestyle adjustments such as scheduled naps.

Prevention

Narcolepsy cannot be prevented, but early diagnosis and treatment can help control symptoms and improve quality of life.
