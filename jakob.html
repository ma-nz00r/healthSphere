Jakob-Creutzfeldt Disease (CJD)

Overview

CJD is a rare, fatal brain disorder caused by abnormal prion proteins leading to brain damage.

Causes

Caused by misfolded prion proteins, can be sporadic, inherited, or acquired.

Symptoms

  • Rapid mental decline
  • Memory loss
  • Personality changes
  • Movement problems

Treatment

No cure exists; supportive care focuses on easing symptoms.

Prevention

Screening of medical instruments and blood donations helps reduce risk.
