Lowe Syndrome

Overview

Lowe Syndrome is a rare genetic disorder affecting the eyes, brain, and kidneys.

Causes

Caused by mutations in the OCRL gene, inherited in an X-linked pattern.

Symptoms

  • Cataracts at birth
  • Developmental delays
  • Weak muscle tone
  • Kidney problems

Treatment

No cure; management includes eye surgery, physical therapy, and kidney care.

Prevention

Genetic counseling can help families understand risks.
