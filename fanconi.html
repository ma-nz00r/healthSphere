Fanconi Anemia

What is Fanconi Anemia?



Fanconi anemia (FA) is a rare genetic disorder that affects the bone marrow, leading to a decrease in the production
of blood cells. This condition increases the risk of leukemia, other cancers, and various birth defects. FA is characterized by defects in DNA repair mechanisms.



Causes of Fanconi Anemia



Fanconi anemia is caused by mutations in genes that are involved in the repair of damaged DNA. These mutations lead to
faulty DNA repair, which can cause bone marrow failure and increase the risk of cancer. FA is inherited in an autosomal recessive manner, meaning that both
parents must pass on a mutated gene for their child to develop the condition.



Symptoms of Fanconi Anemia



Bone marrow failure (low red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets)

Physical abnormalities (such as short stature, skin discoloration, and skeletal abnormalities)

Increased risk of cancers, particularly leukemia

Frequent infections and bruising

Delayed growth and development

Treatment for Fanconi Anemia



There is no cure for Fanconi anemia, but treatments aim to manage the symptoms and improve quality of life. Options include:



Bone marrow transplant

Blood transfusions

Chemotherapy or radiation therapy for cancers

Supportive care to manage infections and other complications

Prevention of Fanconi Anemia



Since Fanconi anemia is a genetic disorder, it cannot be entirely prevented. However, genetic counseling is recommended for
individuals with a family history of FA, and early detection through genetic testing
can help manage the condition. Prenatal testing may also be considered in high-risk families.
