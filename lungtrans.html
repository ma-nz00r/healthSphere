Lung Transplant

HealthSphere Approach

Multidisciplinary team approach: Doctors trained in lung conditions (pulmonologists), chest surgery (thoracic surgeons), heart surgery (cardiac surgeons), infectious disease management, mental health (psychiatrists), and other specialties collaborate as a team to provide you with coordinated, comprehensive care. Together with healthcare professionals in many areas, they evaluate you, determine your eligibility, perform your transplant, and coordinate follow-up care.

Individualized Approach

HealthSphere doctors take the time to get to know you and work with you to provide exactly the care you need. Your transplant team discusses your individual needs to decide the most appropriate treatment. Multi-organ transplant experience: HealthSphere offers the full spectrum of options for lung transplantation, including heart-lung transplants. Our doctors and surgeons have expertise treating complex conditions requiring multi-organ transplants.

Bringing Research Advances to Patient Care

Lung disease expertise: HealthSphere lung specialists and transplant surgeons have extensive experience treating a wide variety of complex lung conditions. Surgical expertise: Our surgeons are skilled in single-lung, double-lung, and multi-organ transplant surgeries, with outcomes backed by decades of experience.

The HealthSphere Experience

HealthSphere specialists bring decades of expertise performing lung transplants for adults and children with both cancerous and noncancerous diseases. Thousands of successful procedures highlight our commitment to care, innovation, and compassion.

Research and Innovation

HealthSphere researchers are pioneers in lung regeneration and transplantation. Studies focus on improving surgical techniques, outcomes, and post-transplant therapies. For example, stem cell research explores regenerating lung tissue, while our Lung Restoration Center develops ex vivo lung perfusion techniques to preserve and restore donor lungs. We also advance immunosuppressant medications to prevent rejection and improve patient safety. HealthSphere campuses nationwide are recognized among the top hospitals for respiratory disorders.
