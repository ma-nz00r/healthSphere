Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH)

Overview

Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus is a condition in which cerebrospinal fluid builds up in the brainâ€™s ventricles, causing walking difficulties, memory issues, and bladder problems.

Causes

Can result from head injury, infection, bleeding, or unknown reasons. Often affects older adults.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty walking (shuffling gait)
  • Memory loss and confusion
  • Loss of bladder control
  • Balance problems

Treatment

The main treatment is surgical placement of a shunt to drain excess fluid. Physical and occupational therapy may also help.

Prevention

No specific prevention, but managing head injuries and infections may reduce risk.
